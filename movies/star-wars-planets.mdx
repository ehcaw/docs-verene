# Star Wars planets

The Star Wars galaxy spans thousands of worlds, each with unique environments, cultures, and significance to the saga.

### Coruscant

The city-planet that serves as the political center of the galaxy. Home to the Jedi Temple and the Galactic Senate.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Type** | Ecumenopolis (city-planet) |
| **Climate** | Temperate (climate-controlled) |
| **Notable locations** | Jedi Temple, Senate Building |

### Alderaan

A beautiful, peaceful world destroyed by the Death Star as a demonstration of Imperial power.

## Desert worlds

- **Tatooine** - Luke Skywalker's home world, controlled by the Hutts
- **Jakku** - Rey's home, site of a major Imperial defeat
- **Jedha** - Holy city for Force worshippers

<Info>
Tatooine appears in more Star Wars films than any other planet, serving as a crucial location in both the original and prequel trilogies.
</Info>

## Ice and snow worlds

### Hoth

The Rebel Alliance's Echo Base was located on this frozen planet, featured prominently in *The Empire Strikes Back*.

### Ilum

Sacred to the Jedi, this icy world contains kyber crystal caves where Padawans construct their lightsabers.

## Volcanic and lava worlds

### Mustafar

The fiery planet where Anakin Skywalker and Obi-Wan Kenobi had their legendary duel. Vader later built his fortress here.

## Ocean worlds

- **Kamino** - Stormy ocean planet where the clone army was created
- **Mon Cala** - Home of the Mon Calamari and Admiral Ackbar
- **Ahch-To** - Location of the first Jedi Temple

## Notable locations

| Planet | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| **Naboo** | Padme Amidala's home, Palpatine's origin |
| **Bespin** | Cloud City, site of Luke and Vader's first duel |
| **Scarif** | Imperial data vault, Death Star plans stolen here |
| **Exegol** | Hidden Sith world, Palpatine's return |
| **Mandalore** | Home world of the Mandalorian warriors |

## The Death Star

While not a planet, the Death Star deserves mention as one of the most iconic locations in the saga.

> "That's no moon... it's a space station." — Obi-Wan Kenobi

The Empire constructed two Death Stars, both ultimately destroyed by the Rebel Alliance.
