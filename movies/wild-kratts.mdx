# Wild Kratts

**Wild Kratts** is an American-Canadian educational animated series created by Chris and Martin Kratt. The series follows the adventures of the Kratt brothers as they explore wildlife and learn about animals using special creature power suits.

## Overview

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Created by** | Chris Kratt and Martin Kratt |
| **First episode** | January 3, 2011 |
| **Network** | PBS Kids |
| **Seasons** | 6+ |
| **Episodes** | 140+ |

## Main characters

- **Chris Kratt** - One of the Kratt brothers, wears a green creature power suit
- **Martin Kratt** - The other Kratt brother, wears a blue creature power suit
- **Aviva Corcovado** - The team's inventor who creates the creature power technology
- **Koki** - The team's computer expert and communications specialist
- **Jimmy Z** - The pilot of the Tortuga headquarters

## The creature power suits

The Kratt brothers use special suits that allow them to gain the abilities of different animals. Each suit requires a creature power disc, which Aviva programs with specific animal DNA.

<Info>
The creature power suits are activated by touching an animal and inserting the corresponding power disc.
</Info>

### Featured creatures

The series has featured hundreds of animals including:

1. Cheetahs - The fastest land animals
2. Peregrine falcons - The fastest animals overall
3. Honey badgers - Known for their fearlessness
4. Octopuses - Masters of camouflage

### Villains

The Wild Kratts team often faces villains who threaten wildlife:

- **Zach Varmitech** - An inventor who uses animals for his robots
- **Donita Donata** - A fashion designer who exploits animals
- **Gourmand** - A chef who hunts endangered species
- **Paisley Paver** - Wants to pave over natural habitats

## Cultural impact

Wild Kratts has become one of the most popular educational shows for children, combining entertainment with genuine wildlife education. The series encourages environmental awareness and appreciation for biodiversity.

<Note>
The Kratt brothers previously hosted the live-action series *Kratts' Creatures* (1996) and *Zoboomafoo* (1999-2001).
</Note>
