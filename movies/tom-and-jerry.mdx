# Tom and Jerry

**Tom and Jerry** is an American animated media franchise and series of comedy short films created in 1940 by William Hanna and Joseph Barbera. The series features the endless rivalry between Tom the cat and Jerry the mouse.

## Overview

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Created by** | William Hanna and Joseph Barbera |
| **First short** | February 10, 1940 |
| **Studio** | Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer |
| **Original shorts** | 114 theatrical shorts |
| **Academy Awards** | 7 wins |

## Main characters

- **Tom Cat** - A gray and white domestic shorthair cat who chases Jerry
- **Jerry Mouse** - A small brown house mouse who outsmarts Tom
- **Spike** - A gray bulldog who often defends Jerry
- **Tyke** - Spike's son, a small gray puppy
- **Nibbles/Tuffy** - A small gray mouse, Jerry's nephew

## The classic formula

The series follows a simple but effective premise: Tom perpetually chases Jerry, and Jerry always outsmarts him. Despite the violence in their rivalry, the duo occasionally shows they genuinely care for each other.

<Info>
Tom and Jerry won seven Academy Awards for Best Animated Short Film, more than any other animated series.
</Info>

### Original theatrical shorts (1940-1958)

The Hanna-Barbera era produced 114 shorts and is considered the golden age of Tom and Jerry. Notable shorts include:

1. *Puss Gets the Boot* (1940) - The debut short
2. *The Yankee Doodle Mouse* (1943) - Academy Award winner
3. *Mouse Trouble* (1944) - Academy Award winner
4. *Cat Concerto* (1947) - Academy Award winner

### Television series

The franchise expanded to television with multiple series:

- *The Tom and Jerry Show* (1975)
- *Tom & Jerry Kids* (1990)
- *Tom and Jerry Tales* (2006)
- *The Tom and Jerry Show* (2014)

## Cultural impact

Tom and Jerry revolutionized animation with its fluid movement, expressive characters, and perfectly timed slapstick comedy. The series influenced generations of animators and remains popular worldwide.

<Note>
The series has been dubbed into numerous languages and broadcast in over 80 countries.
</Note>

## Legacy

> "Don't you believe it!"

Tom and Jerry continues to entertain audiences more than 80 years after its creation. The characters have appeared in feature films, television series, video games, and merchandise, cementing their place as cultural icons of animation history.
